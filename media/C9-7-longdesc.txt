The diagram represents the influences on group cohesiveness listed as follows: “Group hegemony,” “Group maturity,” “Group size,” “Frequency of interaction,” “Clear group goals,” “Competition of external threat,” and “Success.” These influences result in high and low group cohesiveness. The consequences of high group cohesiveness are listed as follows: “Continued membership,” “Influence over members,” “Participation and loyalty,” and “Satisfaction.” The consequences of low group cohesiveness are listed as follows: “Higher turnover and absenteeism,” “Member autonomy,” “Isolation, less loyalty,” and “Lower satisfaction.”
