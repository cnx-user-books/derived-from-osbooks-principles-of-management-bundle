The model of management and organizational behavior is divided into three phases labeled as “Organizational Inputs from Environment,” “Organizational Processes,” and “Organizational Outcomes.” In the first phase, the organizational inputs from the environment that lead to organizational processes are labeled as “Capital,” “Human Resources,” “Technology,” “Government Regulations,” “Raw Materials,” and “Economic Conditions.” In the second phase, the organizational processes are listed into five types as “Leadership,” “Communication,” “Decision-making,” “Power and politics,” and “Conflict and negotiation.” It further shows four interrelated factors contributing to organizational forces labeled as “Individuals and groups,” “Tasks and technology,” “Management,” and “Organization design.” The organizational processes lead to the third phase of organizational outcomes. The three organizational outcomes are labeled as “Individual performance,” “Group performance,” and “Organizational effectiveness.”
