The illustration shows the major influences on job-related stress grouped under, “Organizational Influences” and “Personal Influences.” The four factors contributing to organizational influences are “Occupation,” “Role ambiguity,” “Role conflict,” and “Role overload or underutilization.” The four factors contributing to personal influences are “Personal control,” “Type A personality,” “Rate of life change,” and “Locus of control.” The factors related to the buffering effects on work-related stress are “Social support” and “Hardiness.” The three degrees or intensity levels of experienced stress are “No stress,” “Low stress,” and “High stress.”
