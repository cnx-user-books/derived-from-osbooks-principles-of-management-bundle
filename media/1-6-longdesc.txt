The illustration shows different levels of conceptutal, human, and technical skills required at different stages of the managerial hierarchy. From left to right the three levels are labeled as: Executive managers, middle managers, and  first-line managers. Executive managers require more conceptual skills and less technical skills. Middle managers require all the skills, that is, conceptual, human, and technical skills. First-line managers require far more technical skills than conceptual skills. All the three levels require human skills.
